Historical Timeline of Linus Torvalds



December 28, 1969: Linus Torvalds was born in Helsinki, Finland.

1981: At age 10, he starts programming on a Commodore VIC-20, sparking his lifelong passion for computer science.

1991: While studying computer science at the University of Helsinki, he created the Linux kernel, which will revolutionize computing.

1991: Linus releases the first version of Linux, marking the beginning of open-source software development.

1994: Linux version 1.0 is released, marking the beginning of widespread adoption of the operating system. Its open-source nature attracts developers around the globe, who contribute to its growth and development.

2005: Linus creates Git, a version control system that becomes essential for software development worldwide.

2012: Torvalds receives the Millennium Technology Prize from the Technology Academy Finland for his creation of Linux and his role in promoting open-source collaboration, underscoring the global impact of his work.

Present Day: Linus Torvalds continues to oversee the development of Linux, which has grown to power billions of devices, from smartphones running Android to the majority of the world's supercomputers and servers. Linux is foundational to modern computing, influencing the infrastructure of the internet and cloud-based services.
